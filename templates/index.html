Sorting Algorithms: A Deep Dive

Sorting algorithms are used to sort a data structure according to a specific order relationship, such as numerical or lexicographical order. This operation is a cornerstone in the field of computer science, and countless methods have been developed to optimize this procedure.

Nowadays, there are hundreds of different sorting algorithms, each with its unique characteristics. These algorithms are often classified based on their space complexity and time complexity.

These complexities are represented using asymptotic notations, often denoted by O, Θ, and Ω, which respectively represent the upper, tight, and lower bounds of the algorithm's complexity.

Categories of Sorting Algorithms

  • Logarithmic: The complexity is proportional to the binary logarithm of n. An example is Quick sort, with complexities O(n × log n).
  • Quadratic: The complexity is proportional to n2. A quintessential example is Bubble sort, with a time complexity of O(n2).

These complexities can vary depending on different cases: best case, average case, and worst case.
